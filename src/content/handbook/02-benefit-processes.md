## IXN Undergraduate or Graduate Project Collaboration (B01)

### Trigger
A call for IXN project proposals is released to industry partners.

### Actions
- The partner submits a project proposal through the standard IXN form.
- Module leaders review proposals for suitability and alignment with learning outcomes.
- Module leaders approve or decline proposals based on academic fit and student capacity.
- The Strategic Alliances Team (SAT) confirms acceptance and initiates a framework agreement if one is not already in place.
- The framework agreement is negotiated and signed between the partner and UCL.
- Student letters and project confirmation letters are issued.
- The project is assigned to a student team and an academic supervisor.

### Outcome
The student project begins following the academic module schedule, with regular supervision and agreed deliverables.

## Brand Visibility on the UCL Computer Science Website (B02)

### Trigger
The partner is confirmed as a member of Friends of UCL Computer Science.

### Actions
- The Strategic Alliances Team collects brand assets and a short organisation profile.
- Content is reviewed for compliance with UCL brand and communications guidelines.
- The organisation profile is published on the UCL Computer Science website.

### Outcome
The partner organisation appears on the website as a recognised innovation partner.

## Curated Short Courses for Workforce Development (B04)

### Trigger
The partner expresses interest in workforce development opportunities.

### Actions
- The Strategic Alliances Team shares a curated list of relevant short courses.
- The partner selects courses and nominates participants.
- Enrolment and invoicing are coordinated with the delivering department.

### Outcome
Participants gain access to UCL short courses aligned with organisational development goals.
